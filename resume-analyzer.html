Resume Analyzer Auto

Drop a resume or CV — get a full AI audit and a job-targeted document output.

Resume Analyzer runs 15 AI steps on your uploaded resume or CV — no copy-pasting required. After the analysis, the compiled document opens in a live editor where you can refine it and request AI-powered revisions. Once approved, five generators unlock: LinkedIn profile content, a personalized cover letter, a full interview prep package (questions, STAR stories, salary negotiation), post-interview email templates, and a 1-page resume variant. Steps marked Optional are skipped automatically when the relevant input isn't provided. Steps marked System run automatically.

The 15 Analysis Steps
1
Contact & Header
Checks name, professional title line, email, phone, LinkedIn URL, and location format for completeness and ATS safety. For technical roles, also checks for a GitHub profile URL. Flags missing portfolio or professional site links for roles where online work samples matter. Runs a PII risk check — flags date of birth, photos, full home addresses, marital status, or other information that creates legal liability for employers.
2
Core Competencies & Skills
Audits keyword coverage against your target role, flags outdated or vague skills, checks for required credentials and certifications, and reorganizes by category.
3
Role Relevance Triage
Evaluates every role in your history for relevance to the target position. Assigns each a treatment: Full Write, Condensed, Early Career Group, or Drop. Critical for long resumes spanning decades of experience.
4
Transferable Skills
Identifies capabilities demonstrated in one context that apply to the target role but aren't currently framed as relevant. Especially valuable for career changers and non-linear histories.
5
Professional Summary
Rewrites your summary informed by the skills gap analysis, triage decisions, and transferable skills — so it leads with the right credentials, uses relevant keywords, and cuts generic filler.
6
Experience & Accomplishments
Rewrites job entries based on triage decisions. Full Write roles get 3–5 impact bullets. Condensed roles get 1–2 lines. Old irrelevant roles are grouped or dropped. Includes a three-tier achievement quality audit (task-level → outcome-level → impact-level) with targeted questions to help you find missing metrics.
7
Supporting Sections
Reviews Education, Certifications, Projects, Publications, Awards, Language Skills, and Patents for completeness and relevance. CV mode gives full treatment to academic credentials. Includes citation format guidance (APA/MLA by field), language proficiency level standards (Native through Intermediate), patent formatting, and a references guidance note — "References available upon request" is outdated; the prompt advises having a formatted reference sheet ready instead.
8
Career Narrative & Progression
Evaluates whether your career arc tells a coherent story: trajectory, gaps, short tenures, pivots, and whether the progression logically leads to your target role. Includes a red flag check for anything that might raise a recruiter's eyebrow — with specific language to address each issue on the document.
9
Format, Structure & ATS
Evaluates section ordering, date consistency, bullet length, page count, and ATS danger zones like tables, columns, headers/footers, and graphics. Issues here can cause a resume to be silently rejected before a human ever reads it.
10
Tailoring & Keyword AlignmentOptional
Maps your resume against the job description, scores the match 1–10, identifies genuine keyword gaps, and recommends specific language substitutions — without keyword stuffing. Any keyword that can't be authentically backed up in an interview is flagged as a genuine gap, not inserted anyway.
11
Company ResearchOptional
Searches the web in real time for the target company's mission, tech stack, culture, and recent news — then produces resume alignment tips and cover letter hooks specific to that employer. Requires a company name. In LM Studio mode, also requires a web search plugin; skips gracefully if unavailable.
12
Level & Compensation Signals
Evaluates whether your resume projects the right seniority level for the target role by auditing title signals, scope indicators (team size, budget owned), accomplishment scale, and language framing. Also assesses whether the document positions you to negotiate at the top of the compensation range.
13
Consistency & Voice Audit
Reads all prior rewritten sections holistically — not the original resume — to check that the same professional persona, tone, and positioning comes through consistently. Also checks that the career narrative framing is reflected across all sections.
14
Fix ReconciliationSystem
Reads all 13 analysis outputs and compiles every recommended change into a single numbered checklist, categorized by type (Content Rewrite, Keyword Add, Framing Adjust, Structural Fix, Addition, Removal). Ensures nothing gets lost when the final document is built.
15
Build DocumentSystem
Applies every checklist item to produce the final Resume or CV. Uses rewrite-source steps as content and applies recommendations from analysis steps. Followed by a Compliance Notes section listing any checklist items that could not be applied, and a Digital Presence Note.

Generate LinkedIn ContentAfter Approval
Generates six LinkedIn sections: Headline (3 variations), About (first person, 150–200 words, ends with CTA), Experience (all roles, narrative tone, no triage), Skills list (30–50 keywords by category), Featured section recommendations, and Profile Settings — covering Open to Work configuration, three recommendation request templates, and a Profile Completeness checklist (photo, banner, custom URL, 500+ connections, endorsements).
✉
Cover Letter GeneratorAfter Approval
Generates a personalized 300–400 word cover letter. Company-aware when Company Research ran. Four-paragraph structure: opening hook, strongest evidence with metric, secondary strength plus company reference, confident close. First person, specific, never repeats the full resume. Add the hiring manager's name before sending.
◈
Interview Prep & STAR StoriesAfter Approval
Three-part package: (1) 12–15 likely interview questions across four categories with an explanation of what each is testing. (2) 5–6 STAR stories from your strongest achievements with Situation, Task, Action, Result, and guidance on which questions each answers. (3) Salary negotiation and offer evaluation — positioning statement, leverage points, timing guidance, counter-offer language, beyond-base negotiables, and an offer evaluation framework for when the offer arrives.
@
Email TemplatesAfter Approval
Three post-interview templates: same-day thank-you note (warm, genuine, under 150 words), follow-up email after 7–10 days of silence (confident, not desperate, under 100 words), and a LinkedIn connection request to the interviewer (personal, not a pitch, under 250 characters). Fill in specific details before sending.
⊡
1-Page Resume VariantIn Editor
Available any time in the editor — Resume mode only. Generates an aggressively trimmed single-page version: most recent 10 years, 2–3 bullets per role, essential sections only. Opens in the 1-Page Version tab alongside the full document. Not available for CVs — a 1-page CV defeats its purpose.
What to Fill In
✓
Document type
Choose Resume (1–2 pages, role-targeted) or Full CV (comprehensive, no length limit) before uploading. If you're unsure, click the "Not sure which to choose?" link below the selector buttons — it explains both formats and when each is appropriate. This choice affects every step: triage rules, experience depth, skills breadth, page limits, supporting section treatment, and the compiled output.
✓
Industry & Target Role
Used by every step to calibrate language, keywords, and relevance decisions. Be specific — 'Purchasing Agent' and 'Industrial Manufacturing' produces far more targeted output than 'Operations' and 'Manufacturing'.
✓
Job Description
Paste the full posting to unlock Step 10 (Tailoring & Keyword Alignment). When present, every other step also aligns to the JD's language, keywords, and priorities.
✓
Company Name
Unlocks Step 11 (Company Research). The AI searches the web in real time — use the company's exact public name for best results. Company research also feeds into the Cover Letter Generator (personalizing the employer references) and the Interview Prep (company-specific questions and talking points).
✓
Your File
PDF or Word document (.pdf, .docx). Text is extracted automatically in the browser before anything is sent to the AI. Use the 'Preview extracted text' toggle to verify the extraction looks clean before running — especially important for complex PDFs.
✓
Mode
Claude (default) or LM Studio (local model). LM Studio requires a running server with a 16K+ context window model — 13B+ parameters recommended. The Reconcile and Compile steps send large inputs. Company Research requires a web search plugin in LM Studio; skips gracefully without one.
⏱
Expect 5–10 minutes
Each step is a separate API call. A full run with all 15 steps typically takes 5–10 minutes. Company Research (Step 11) takes longest due to live web searching.
📄
File quality matters
Text-based PDFs extract cleanly. Scanned, image-based, or heavily designed files (columns, tables, graphics) may extract poorly. Use a plain Word doc or a standard-format PDF for best results. Always check the extraction preview.
✏️
Review before approving
The AI rewrites are strong but not infallible. Check every date, metric, job title, and technical claim before approving. You can edit directly or describe changes in plain language — as many passes as you need.
🔒
Authenticity is enforced
The AI will never change the industry context of a role (a retail job stays retail), never invent metrics, and never insert keywords you can't back up in an interview. Genuine gaps are flagged, not papered over. The compiled document also includes a background check reminder — every date, title, and credential must match what a background check will return.
💾
Save as you go
Use the Copy buttons on each analysis section, the document editor, and each generator output to grab what you need. Nothing is saved between sessions — copy everything before closing the tab.
📋
Resume vs CV
A resume is 1–2 pages, tailored per application, standard for most US/UK private sector jobs. A CV is comprehensive, has no page limit, and is standard in academia, medicine, research, and international hiring. Click "Not sure which to choose?" below the selector buttons for full guidance.
🔁
Re-run any time
Change the target role, swap the job description, try a different company, then click Analyze again. Each run is fully independent. Use the New Resume button to start fresh with a different file.
📄 Resume
  • 1–2 pages maximum — ruthlessly focused on relevance
  • Tailored to a specific job posting or role type
  • Older or less relevant roles condensed or removed
  • Summary is 3–5 lines of tight positioning
  • Skills limited to the 10–15 most relevant keywords
  • Supporting sections included only if they serve the role
  • Publications, patents, and research rarely included
  • Standard in most private sector hiring in the US and UK
📋 Full CV (Curriculum Vitae)
  • No page limit — completeness over conciseness
  • A permanent, comprehensive record of your entire career
  • Every role included regardless of age or relevance
  • Publications, presentations, research, and grants get full treatment
  • Broader skills section including research methods and academic competencies
  • Education section typically near the top (not buried below experience)
  • Standard in academia, research, medicine, and international hiring
  • A single CV is updated over time, not rewritten per application
Use a Resume when: applying for most private sector jobs in the US, Canada, or UK — corporate, tech, finance, retail, manufacturing, non-profit, government. If the job posting doesn't specify, a resume is almost always correct.

Use a CV when: applying for academic positions (professor, researcher, postdoc), medical or clinical roles that require a full credentials record, research-focused roles at universities or institutes, international positions (CVs are standard in most of Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East), or any role that specifically asks for a CV or curriculum vitae.

Not sure? If you're in the US and applying to a non-academic employer, choose Resume. If you have extensive publications, research experience, or are applying internationally or in academia, choose CV.
