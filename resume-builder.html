Resume Builder AI

Build a high-quality resume or CV from scratch — job by job, industry by industry

Click "Check Connection" to verify LM Studio is ready

Resume Builder helps you capture your full work history and generate strong, industry-specific bullet points for each role — without needing an existing resume. Work through your jobs one at a time. For each role, the AI either generates bullets from your description or runs an expert interview to pull out the details that make strong resume content. When you're done, build either a complete Master Document or a date-trimmed Recent History version. Neither output is application-ready — run it through Resume Analyzer to target it for a specific job.

The Four Steps
1
Personal Information
Your name, contact details, LinkedIn URL, and optional portfolio or GitHub link. No target role or job description here — the builder isn't about targeting, it's about capturing your history accurately.
2
Work History
Add each role with its own industry tag. This is the core of the builder — each role is written independently using the language and standards of that specific industry. A role in retail purchasing gets retail purchasing bullets. A role in manufacturing operations gets manufacturing operations bullets. The industry tag travels with the role, not the document.
3
Supporting Sections
Education, certifications, skills (rough list — AI organizes into categories), and optional extras. CV mode unlocks Publications, Languages with standard proficiency levels, and additional academic sections.
4
Build Document
Choose Master Document (every role, complete history) or Recent History (last 10 or 15 years, trimmed by date only — no AI judgment about relevance). The document opens in a review editor where you can make changes and request revisions before exporting.
The Expert Interview
?
When it activates
If your role description is under 40 words, the expert interview activates automatically before generating bullets. 40 words is roughly two or three short sentences — not enough for the AI to produce strong, specific content. You can also start the interview manually on any role by clicking "Expert Interview" after generating bullets.
?
What it asks
The AI generates targeted questions based on the job title and industry — not generic questions. A fast food crew member gets questions about volume, speed, and customer handling. A restaurant manager gets questions about labor cost, food cost, and scheduling. A manufacturing supervisor gets questions about throughput, downtime, and safety. The number of questions varies: 3–4 for entry-level roles, 5–6 for mid-level, 6–8 for senior or executive.
?
How answers are used
Your answers inform the bullet generation but never appear verbatim in the document. The AI uses them to write strong, specific, metric-backed bullets in proper resume format. Answer naturally — no need to write in resume language. The messier and more specific your answers, the better the output. You can skip any question or skip the whole interview and generate from your description alone.
✓
Approving a role
After bullets are generated, review and refine them using the feedback field — describe what to change and the AI will revise only what you requested. When you're happy, click Approve. The sidebar dot turns green and that role is locked into the final document. You can come back and unapprove any role to make more changes before building.
Build Options
📋
Master Document
Every role included, fully written, no cuts. Your complete professional record. Good for long-term record keeping, as input for the analyzer, or as a reference document. If you're building a CV, this is essentially what a CV is — use this option.
📄
Recent History (10 or 15 Years)
Same bullets, same quality — just trimmed by date. No AI judgment about relevance, just a cutoff. 10 years is standard for most resume formats. 15 years is better for senior roles or fields where earlier experience still matters. Roles with no parseable end date are kept.
⚠
Neither output is application-ready
The document produced here is a high-quality starting point — not something to send to a recruiter. It hasn't been tailored to a specific job, checked for ATS compatibility, or aligned to a job description. Run it through Resume Analyzer before submitting anywhere. The analyzer will do the targeting, keyword alignment, triage, and final polish.
🏭
Industry per role matters
Each role gets its own industry tag — not a document-level setting. Someone who worked in retail, then moved to healthcare administration, then to manufacturing procurement gets bullets that sound right for each job's world.
📏
40-word threshold
40 words is about two short sentences. Anything less and the AI doesn't have enough to work from — the expert interview fills that gap. More detail always produces better bullets, interview or not.
🔢
Numbers matter most
The AI will never invent metrics. Where numbers are missing, it writes [add your number] as a placeholder. The expert interview questions are specifically designed to help you remember the numbers — dollar amounts, percentages, team sizes, volumes.
📋
CV vs Resume
Resume: industry-specific bullets, standard format, good starting point for targeted applications. CV: same bullets plus publications, languages with proficiency levels, academic sections. Switch the toggle before starting — it affects what fields appear and how content is generated.
💾
Nothing is saved
All data lives in this browser session. Nothing is stored between sessions. Use the Copy button or Save as PDF to preserve your work before closing the tab. If you close the tab, start over.
➡
Next step: Analyzer
Once you have a document you're happy with, open Resume Analyzer. Paste or upload the document, enter a target role and job description, and let the analyzer tailor it, fix ATS issues, and produce a polished final version ready to submit.
